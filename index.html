Week 3 Recap: Blowouts Everywhere

Big wins, ugly pitching lines, and a few teams already making serious statements. Here is the full league recap for Week 3 of UTI Fantasy Baseball.

By: Commish Gunnarrhea Week: 3 Feature: Matchup-by-matchup recap

Week 3 came with loads of blowouts, a few early-season statements, and some teams (This is Mizerable) that may already need to start asking bigger questions. Before we get into the matchup recaps, we have to hand out the weekly hardware.

Weekly 2 Weekly Winner Surprising Gunnarrhea takes it this week with 10 NSB. That is wild considering they had just 6 stolen bases last week while Goodyear Gila Monsters posted 20. But hey, that’s showbiz baby!!

New Weekly Category: Total Bases

BTA Boys vs This is Mizerable

Result: BTA Boys stay undefeated and looked dominant doing it.
BTA Boys 12.5 – 3.5 This is Mizerable

This one was typed up after Monday night due to the BTA Boys absolutely dominating, embarrassing and creaming all over This is Mizerable. Guys and Pauli G, I want to emphasize that this was an absolute shit show. At one point, BTA Boys were up 16-0, and there was real discussion about whether This is Mizerable would even get a single category.

It is still early in the season, but it feels pretty safe to say This is Mizerable is still deep in rebuild mode. On the other side, BTA Boys are looking like one of the teams to beat. Three weeks in, they are still undefeated, and the confidence around this roster is only growing.

League takeaway: BTA Boys are the real deal, and apparently all it took was the responsible decision to actually start Paul Skenes on pitching days.

Me So Heorny vs Goodyear Gila Monsters

Result: Another undefeated team survives a heavyweight matchup.
Me So Heorny 11.5 – 4.5 Goodyear Gila Monsters

This had all the makings of one of the great matchups of the week, and Me So Heorny came out in control and never really gave the Goodyear Gila Monsters a chance to turn it into a close one. Even though the final score looks lopsided, several hitting categories were actually pretty close and could have swung the other way.

The difference ended up being on the mound, where Me So Heorny absolutely crushed. Even if some of the hitting categories flipped, it would have been enough due to Goodyear’s atrocious pitching line: a 6.02 ERA, .271 BAA, and 1.50 WHIP. Honestly, it reminds me of Jake Bramble's pitching stats back at Westside. But that is now two straight weeks of ugly pitching numbers for a team that expects to contend. If i was Goodyear, I'd think of trading Skubal. Wouldn't want to waste his prime on such a mid pitching staff.

League takeaway: Me So Heorny looks like a complete, well-rounded team. As for Goodyear, if they want to get back to the ship, they may need to make some real pitching upgrades fast and not act like Jake Bramble at Westside.

BB’s Bold Team vs You Don’t Know Bo

Result: The runner-up keeps rolling while Bo keeps searching.
BB’s Bold Team 11.5 – 4.5 You Don’t Know Bo

Our final undefeated team and 2025 Runner Up, BB’s Bold Team, took down the 2025 GM of the Year, You Don’t Know Bo, 11.5 - 4.5. BB’s Bold Team might not have had their sharpest week, but it was easily enough to take down Bo. Through three weeks, BB’s Bold Team is doing exactly what a top-tier contender is supposed to do: stack wins early, control categories, and keep themselves right near the top of the standings.

Bo still has not found a win yet this season as his team continues to really struggle. But being the 2025 GM of the Year, we know he’ll turn it around… lol SIKE. Like Goodyear, they better look at finding some more pitching if they want to compete. Only putting up 36 innings this week is tough, especially when the pitchers have been struggling as much as Bo’s as they put up a 5:50 ERA, 1.63 WHIP and .308 BAA. And again, reminds me of Bramble’s pitching stats over at Westside. But the once dominant Crochet is looking like hot garbage and Sasaki needs to go back to Japan. And offensively, Nick Kurtz is looking like a bum, Springer needs to retire, and the $200 spent on auction for Otto Lopez MIGHT not have been worth it. Better look at that waiver this week!

League takeaway: Bo still has not found a win yet this season, and the early hole is getting harder to ignore. BB is looking to repeat championship appearances

Dixon Cider vs Mactown MacDaddies

Result: The defending champion handles business.
Dixon Cider 10.5 – 5.5 Mactown MacDaddies

The 2025 defending champion, Dixon Cider, put together another solid week and took down the struggling Mactown MacDaddies by a comfortable margin. Dixon Cider’s offense is finally looking like they're back in shape as they hit 16 homeruns, 65 runs , 48 walks, and 145 Total Bases. Pretty elite numbers to go along with very solid pitching that is highlighted by 92 strikeouts and a .196 BAA.

The Mactown MacDaddies are another team that is yet to find a win and continue to put up mid stats each week. The numbers aren't terrible but when you're competing against the bgidawgs, you better bring your A game! The MacDaddies just neeed to get thie volume up as the had 276 ABs to Dixon Ciders 305 and 55 IPs to Dixon Ciders 84

League takeaway: Dixon Cider may be getting back into championship form as the champ is still doing champ things.

The Big Gay John’s Super Team vs Gunnarrhea

Result: A close call, but disaster was avoided.
The Big Gay John’s Super Team 9 – 7 Gunnarrhea

This was the closest matchup of the slate, and The Big Gay John’s Super Team narrowly avoided what would have been a baaaaad case of Gunnarrhea. In the end, they escaped with a 9-7 win, but this was a battle much tighter than some of the other Week 3 results. John’s Super Team dominated hitting where Gunnarrhea took the pitching

Pitching was an area John’s Super Penis stuggled last year. But with the pickup of the top pitcher in baseball Jose Soriano and his consistent dominant performances, he was able to steal the WHIP category and take the win. But it’s only a matter of time until Soriano beats his boyfriend and Johnboy is back to having a struggling rotation.

League takeaway: Gunnarrhea did not come away with the matchup win, but they still managed to take home the Weekly 2 Weekly crown with 10 NSB. But who gives a fuck. It's shot time for Gunnarrhea
