BB's Bold Team vs BTA Boyz

Result: BB’s Bold Team didn’t even break a sweat — a cruise-control win against a BTA squad that never showed up.
BB's Bold Team 10.5 – 5.5 BTA Boyz

BB’s Bold Team keeps rolling as they cruise past the struggling BTA Boyz 10.5–5.5 to improve to 5–1 and hold down 2nd place. It wasn’t their most dominant showing, but against an ice-cold opponent, they didn’t need to be. The story of the week was volume—BB’s piled up 300 at-bats compared to just 212 from BTA Boyz, making it nearly impossible to compete across hitting categories.

BTA Boyz showed some life early in the season, but over the past month they’ve completely stalled out and look to have gone on autopilot. To be fair, we get it—life’s busy with kids and long work days, and that comes first. But if BTA Boyz want to get back to a competitive level, a mini rebuild is needed. The lack of roster moves, poor IL management, and a pitching staff that somehow racked up nine losses in a single week have really hurt them. Turning things around starts with cleaning up the roster and actually working the waiver wire—because right now, this team looks like it’s in desperate need of some serious TLC. But i must say they are finally on the right track by finally ripping their first shot of the season. Only like 4 more now lol

League takeaway: Volume still wins in this league. BB’s didn’t need a monster performance—they just showed up and outworked their opponent. Meanwhile, BTA Boyz are a reminder that even a decent roster can fall apart fast without active management.

When you’re up against one of the league’s most dangerous offenses, “respectable” just isn’t enough—you’ve got to match firepower, and this week, Goodyear brought far more of it. And I honestly would have thought that Goodyear pitching staff would start crumbling away with the loss of ace Tarik Skubal, but it still looks VERY sharp

This is Mizerable vs Dixon Cider

Result: Dixon Cider flips the script — pitching dominates and the champ reminds everyone why they’re still the team to beat.
Dixon Cider 10 – 6 This is Mizerable

Aaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaand the frontrunner for the Roch Cholowsky pick stays red hot as they lose their 6th game in a row. This one being to the 2025 reigning champion, Dixon Cider. Dixon Cider was in control all week and this no shock to anyone. But what's actually a shock is that throughout the past, Dixon Cider was feared for their scary offesnive power. Now the entire thing is flipped.

Dixon Cider put up VERY average hitting stats but posted 70 innings with an absolutely insane 1.28 ERA, 0.995 WHIP, .176 BAA, and 83 strikeouts. Guys... this is over 70 fucking innings pitched. That is absolutely INSANE!!!! That shit was like my pitching numbers in career at Westside. But holy shit.

To make it even tougher, This Is Mizerable didn’t even have a bad week. They posted a strong 2.29 ERA, 1.19 WHIP, .195 BAA, and 69 Ks, along with above-average hitting numbers. Against most teams, that’s probably a win. But this week? Not against Dixon Cider. At this point, it’s starting to feel like the fantasy gods have already decided that Roch might just be destined to be Mizerable.

League takeaway: Championship teams find multiple ways to win. Dixon proving they don’t need elite hitting to dominate is a scary sign for the league. Meanwhile, This Is Mizerable keeps stacking solid weeks with nothing to show for it—at some point, bad luck turns into a lost season, and that Roch pick is looking more and more inevitable.
